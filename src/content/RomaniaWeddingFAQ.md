## 💍 Wedding FAQ

### When should I arrive?

**Please arrive the day before the wedding.** The wedding day will be long and packed and pulling an all-nighter the day before is not wise. Besides, Oradea is worth exploring  even if just for a day. 


### What does the day look like?

**Church ceremony → Photos around the city → Reception at Camelot Resort → Sunrise**

That last part is not a typo. Romanian weddings go until everyone drops. To quote our DJ "I will e at your wedding whether you will be there or not...and I will be playing for as long as one of you is still standing". Come ready for a full, joyful, ridiculous amount of fun. There will be multiple courses of food, toasts, open bar, candy bar, dancing, more food, more dancing, more games. Do not book an early checkout. Do not schedule anything the next morning.

### What should I wear?

**Smart formal** — think suits, cocktail dresses, evening gowns. Romanians dress *up* for weddings and we love that. You won't be overdressed.  Most importantly - be you, confident and comfortable in your own skin - just a more refined version.

**What NOT to wear:** No full white or ivory — that's the bride's territory and Romanians take it seriously. All-black head to toe is also traditionally associated with mourning. A pop of color never hurts at a Romanian wedding!


### What other traditions should I know about?

**Grăitor**  - Don't be alarmed! The **Grăitor** is the leader of the serenade — the head of the groom's party who arrives singing outside the bride's door. It's joyful, it's theatrical, it's the beginning of the party and it's one of our favorite parts of the day. Clap along, cheer, enjoy the spectacle - it will be fun even if you don't speak the language.

**Traditional Dances** — at some point during the reception the dancefloor opens up for Romanian circle dances — the *hora* and others. You do not need to know the steps. Grab the hand of the person next to you and keep moving. Romanian guests will guide you. Jump in.

**The Shoe Game** — we'll each hold one of our own shoes and one of each other's. Questions about our relationship get asked — who's the better cook? Who said "I love you" first? — and we answer by raising the relevant shoe. It's funny, revealing, and the crowd gets very involved - bring your burning question.

### Will I understand what's going on?

Mostly yes! With a mix of local and international guests, we'll make sure key moments are explained as they happen. But even when things are in Romanian, the mood is always universal. Our Romanian guests will be delighted to adopt you for the evening.

### Any useful phrases for the day?

Just one really gets you everywhere:

> 🥂 ***Noroc!*** — pronounced *Noh-ROCK* — means "Cheers!" Use it every single toast, make eye contact, and you will instantly belong.

A few more that will earn you goodwill:
- *Bună ziua* — Good day (formal hello)
- *Mulțumesc* — Thank you (mool-tsoo-MESK)
- *Felicitări!* — Congratulations
- *Casă de piatră!* - "Stone house" - traditional way if which the bride and the groom a long happy marriage together
- *La mulți ani!* - "To many years" - generic greating used for many occassional, from New Year's Eve, to Birthdays and Weddings.
- *Sărutmâna!* - "Kiss hand" - a greeting primarily addressed to the elderly or a very polite way a man could greet a woman he is not closed to.

## 🗺️ Where to Explore

You've come this far — make the most of it! Here are our top picks before and after the wedding.

### Oradea Itself 🏛️

Check out our [travel blog](https://www.catalam.com/blog/oradea).

### Băile Felix 🧖

Just 8km from the city, this thermal spa resort is the perfect way to recover the day after the wedding. Warm outdoor pools, affordable entry, deeply relaxing. Highly recommended.

### Cluj-Napoca 🎓

Romania's coolest city — young, vibrant, great food and a buzzing café scene. Worth a night or two if you're flying in that way.
If you are taking a trip there, consider seeing the **Turda Salt Mines** nearby.

### Budapest 🇭🇺

Only 2.5 hours away and one of Europe's most beautiful cities. If you haven't been, this is your excuse. Many guests will fly in via Budapest — arriving a day or two early to explore is strongly recommended.

### Vienna

If you are looking for day trips before or after the wedding, consider Budapest (3-4 hr drive) and Vienna (~8 drive from Oradea, but 4hr drive from Budapest).

### The Carpathian Mountains 🏔️

If you have more time, the Carpathians are spectacular — castles, hiking, medieval towns like Sighișoara, Brașov and Sinaia. A road trip through Transylvania is something you'll never forget. Dracula's castle is genuinely there. Yes, really. 

### Maramureș

A Romanian county where traditions are kept. If you wanna laugh in death's face - consider seeing **Cimitirul Vesel** (Happy Cemetery).


> Tip: for the good, the bad and the ugly - check this [Anthony Bourdain's No Reservation - Transylvania episode](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7uQvfCMnC_k) (unfortunate choice of a guide).

## Service ( Hair, Makeup etc)

Beauty services in Oradea are excellent quality and a fraction of what you'd pay at home. **Book early** — the best spots fill up fast around weddings, especially on the day before.

> ⚡ **Top tip:** As soon as you know your travel dates, lock in your appointments. Don't assume you can walk in.

> 💵 **Cash reminder:** Many smaller salons prefer or exclusively take cash. Bring RON.

Romanian ladies are very well taken care of - plenty of hair, make-up and nail salons. Guys get their share as well.
